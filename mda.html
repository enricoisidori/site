Humans have completed the migration into the metaverse and are no longer in contact with the real world. They inhabit digital three-dimensional mdaces that are becoming distant from the real world as the years go by. There is a desire to go and recover objects that have never been scanned by photogrammetry and thus have come so far and observable in space, because they are not deemed worthy. Thus, a digital archaeology initiative is launched through which artificial intelligences perform data mining and web scraping to reconstruct real-world everyday objects that have not arrived in the metaverse and form a digital museum accessible to visitors. Specifically, AIs surface textual materials, images and videos and with other AIs reconstruct from photographs or 2D drawings or even from textual prompts 3D objects to be displayed in the metaverse as heritage to be safeguarded.

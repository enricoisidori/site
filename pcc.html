Post-Computing Lab
Antidisciplinary Research Network & Platform

The Post-Computing Lab is an antidisciplinary research network & platform, a cross-departmental initiative led by MDEF (Master in Design for Emergent Futures) at Elisava School of Design.

This platform reimagines research and education in a world where computation is ambient, embodied, and entangled with life. It serves as a catalyst for radical inquiry, speculative design, and collaborative experimentation at the edge of art, technology, and philosophy.

Operating across Elisava Bachelor and Master programs and anchored by a six-month curriculum from January to June 2026, the platform invites students and faculty to engage in a post-computing civilization centered on themes such as AI, neural media, intimate technologies, and synthetic ecologies.

This is an extra-curricular program designed for students who want to develop their final year project within its radical framework, culminating in a global summit in June in Barcelona.

Key Pillars

  • Antidisciplinary ResearchBreaking disciplinary silos to merge science, design, philosophy, and emerging technologies.
  • Speculative PrototypingCreating tangible future scenarios that challenge conventional assumptions.
  • Experimental CollaborationBringing together institutions, researchers, artists, designers, and technologists to co-create new knowledge.

We are actively seeking student projects that align with and explore the following critical themes:

  • Neural MediaThe fusion of AI, neural interfaces, and immersive environments.
  • Intimate TechnologiesComputing in, on, around the body.
  • NBIC ConvergenceThe convergence of nanotechnology, biotechnology, information technology, and cognitive science.
  • Biology <> TechnologyBrain organoids and Organoid Intelligence.
  • Ethical & Social ImplicationsThe risks and responsibilities of a world where life itself is programmable.
  • Planetary InterfacesHow computation operates across scales from the microscopic to the planetary.
  • Synthetic EcologiesDesigning with and for post-natural systems.

Program Architecture

This is an extra-curricular program designed for students who want to develop their final year project within its framework. It does not grant CFU credits. Participation is free of charge, upon selection.

  • CycleJanuary – June 2026
  • Participants10 – 15 selected participants from MDEF and Elisava Bachelor/Master programs, alongside those from partner institutions.
  • DeliveryMonthly studios, labs, or thesis work.
  • EngagementIn-person in Barcelona and online.

Laboratory

About

The Lab treats communication design as a tool for epistemic resistance. We don’t design decoration; we design situations that question how knowledge is produced and circulated through images, interfaces, and media systems.

Students work in small groups to construct speculative worlds, fictional brands, and interactive prototypes that expose the politics behind everyday communication.

Method

From research to Communicative Machines

Our process overlaps exploration, speculation, visual communication, and prototyping. It is iterative and messy on purpose: the goal is not to “solve” problems, but to reframe them.

Each project moves through five interconnected phases: Exploration, Speculation, Communication, Interaction, and Realization.

The outcome is a Communicative Machine: an interactive artifact that acts as a narrative gateway into the speculative scenario and a provocation for public debate.

Research

Position

The Lab develops research on antidisciplinary design, speculative communication, and critical pedagogy. We are interested in how design can resist purely commercial roles and instead act as a tool to question norms, values, and power structures.

Our publications connect theory and practice, using case studies, student projects, and communicative machines as research devices.

Models

Process diagrams

The Lab’s research also includes process modelling: overlapping double diamonds, design cones, and network-based diagrams that describe how projects move across domains and decision nodes.

These visual models are descriptive, not prescriptive: they map how projects actually evolve in practice, including dead ends, detours, and unexplored spaces.

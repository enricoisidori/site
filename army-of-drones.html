Army of Drones Bonus

Gamified kill-chain incentives for frontline drone crews

Ukraine's military is turning to incentive schemes borrowed from online shooters to spur operators to eliminate more Russian armor. Flights logged through the Army of Drones bonus program earn E-pointswhenever a crew uploads verifiable gun-camera evidence that their quadcopter or FPV drone hit a Russian target.

Those points flow into the national leaderboard and sync with the Brave 1 Market — a procurement portal that functions like an internal Amazon, allowing high-performing platoons to order upgrades as soon as the strikes are confirmed.

Today's briefing tracks the most active crews. Metrics update in real time as units submit footage and central command validates hits via AI classifiers, SIGINT overlays, and human analysts. Approved kills move units higher on the national board, while rejected claims dock points and suspend market access for 12 hours.

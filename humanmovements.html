The photos were taken autonomously by a security camera in my living room for a week whenever it detected human movement. Originally designed for security, the camera’s function evolves into documenting daily life, revealing habits, schedules, and moments from a fixed perspective. These images, often dismissed as accidental byproducts or digital residues, are reframed as artistic artifacts. The pricing is based on a rarity index, derived from tags like time of day and the number of people detected.
